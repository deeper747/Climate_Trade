Notes. US-data tabs report domestic exports (FAS value, re-exports excluded) from UN Comtrade, latest available month March 2026, and general imports (customs value) from the US Census Bureau international trade API, latest available month April 2026. The EU aggregate is the Census Bureau's European Union country grouping of 27 member states. The CBAM-scope tab reports EU27 imports from the United States from Eurostat Comext, restricted to the CN codes covered by Regulation (EU) 2023/956; latest available month is February 2026. Comext values are CIF in euros and are not directly comparable in level with the FAS dollar figures on the US-data tabs. On the US-data tabs, CBAM coverage is approximated at the HS chapter and heading level: HS 73 includes many articles outside CBAM scope and should be read as an upper bound, and HS 31 includes phosphatic and potassic fertilizers that CBAM does not cover. Electricity, the sixth CBAM sector, is omitted because there is no US–EU electricity trade. All values are nominal and reflect both prices and volumes. In weight view, US-data figures are gross shipping weight from the Census Bureau (vessel plus air, in metric tonnes, through April 2026), which captures effectively all US–EU trade since there is no land route; shipping weight covers total exports because the Census Bureau does not separate re-exported goods by weight. Comext quantities are net mass. Gross and net weights are not directly comparable.

Sources. UN Comtrade, US domestic exports; US Census Bureau, International Trade API; Eurostat, EU trade since 2002 by CN8 (Comext DS-045409). Data accessed June 11, 2026.
